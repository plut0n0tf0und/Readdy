FinTrack: Personal Finance Management App

Problem Statement

Users struggle to track multiple financial accounts and visualize spending patterns in an intuitive way.

Solution

A unified dashboard with AI-powered insights and customizable visualization tools for personal finance management.

Duration

12 Weeks

Team Size

4 Members

Timeline

January - March 2025

My Role

Lead UX Designer

User Research

Identifying user pain points in personal finance management

Research Methodology

Our research approach combined qualitative and quantitative methods to gain comprehensive insights into users' financial management behaviors and needs.

User Interviews

Conducted 15 in-depth interviews with target users across different income brackets and financial literacy levels.

Survey Analysis

Collected 250 responses to identify common pain points and feature preferences in financial apps.

Competitive Analysis

Evaluated 8 competing finance apps to identify market gaps and best practices.

Usability Testing

Conducted 3 rounds of testing with 6 participants each to refine our solution.

Key Findings

  • 78% of users struggle to maintain a comprehensive view of their finances across multiple accounts
  • Visual representation of spending patterns was the most requested feature (92% of respondents)
  • Users preferred automated categorization with manual override options
  • Privacy concerns were significant, with 65% expressing hesitation about linking all accounts
  • Notification fatigue was common among existing finance app users

We conducted multiple rounds of usability testing to validate our design decisions and identify areas for improvement.

Key Findings & Iterations

Finding 1: Budget Creation Complexity

Users found the budget creation process too complex with too many required fields.

Solution:

Simplified the budget creation flow with smart defaults and progressive disclosure of advanced options.

Finding 2: Transaction Categorization

Users struggled to find where to recategorize transactions.

Solution:

Added a visible category indicator with edit icon directly in the transaction list view.

Finding 3: Dashboard Information Overload

Some users felt overwhelmed by the amount of information on the dashboard.

Solution:

Implemented a customizable dashboard with the ability to hide/show widgets based on user preference.

Key Learnings

Progressive Disclosure is Essential

The implementation of progressive disclosure principles significantly improved user comprehension and reduced cognitive load, particularly for complex financial features.

Visualization Trumps Raw Data

Users consistently preferred visual representations of financial data over numerical tables, leading us to prioritize chart development and data visualization throughout the app.

Balance Between Automation and Control

Finding the right balance between automated features (like transaction categorization) and user control was crucial for building trust and ensuring accuracy.

Future Considerations

  • Develop a web application to complement the mobile experience
  • Integrate with additional financial services and investment platforms
  • Implement advanced data visualization options for power users
  • Explore social features for shared financial goals and group expenses

Personal Reflection

"Leading the UX design for FinTrack was a rewarding challenge that pushed me to balance technical complexity with user-centered design principles. I'm particularly proud of how we used progressive disclosure to make advanced features accessible without sacrificing simplicity for new users."
